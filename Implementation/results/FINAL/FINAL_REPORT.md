## Section C — Ablation (which new gate matters?)

[section_C_ablation/](section_C_ablation/) — under stealth random-
direction 15× attack, toggle each NEW gate independently.

| Configuration | Acc | F1 | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| acc-only (Firdaus baseline) | 0.850 | 0.830 | 53 acc-rejections; only 5 hit attacker |
| **+ norm only** | **0.683** | **0.627** | over-rejects honest non-IID updates (20 norm-rejections all from H0 by chance) |
| + rep only | 0.850 | 0.830 | rep alone redundant (no signal to track) |
| + DP only | 0.850 | 0.830 | DP doesn't catch poisoning (it's confidentiality, not integrity) |
| **full (norm + rep + DP + acc)** | **0.850** | 0.816 | 29/30 attacker rejections |

**Plot:** `ablation.png`.

**One-line reading:** **norm-bound alone is too aggressive** on non-IID
data (over-rejects honest updates); only the **combination** with the
accuracy gate works. Defense-in-depth is necessary, not optional.

## Section D — Byzantine-edge tolerance (PBFT, n=4)

[section_D_byzantine/](section_D_byzantine/) — Hybrid (Full Proposal)
under different Byzantine configurations of edge servers.

| Scenario | Acc | F1 | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 Byzantine (clean) | **0.850** | 0.836 | ✓ baseline |
| 1/4 silent (within f=1) | **0.850** | 0.836 | ✓ tolerated, chain advances 11 blocks |
| 1/4 lying-prepare (within f=1) | **0.850** | 0.836 | ✓ tolerated, chain advances 11 blocks |
| **2/4 silent (exceeds f=1)** | **0.467** | 0.636 | ✓ **safely halts** (consensus_fail=10, chain blocks=1) |

**Plot:** `byzantine_edge.png`.

**One-line reading:** PBFT delivers Byzantine fault tolerance up to
f=1 of n=4 replicas; safely halts at f=2 rather than producing a
wrong global model. **This is the cleanest empirical win over both
review papers** — neither has it.

## Section E1 — Multi-seed Cleveland (5 seeds)

[section_E1_multiseed/](section_E1_multiseed/) — predictive parity
with statistical confidence.

| Variant | Accuracy (mean ± std) | F1 (mean ± std) | AUC | Time (s) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Only Blockchain | 0.830 ± 0.014 | 0.801 ± 0.017 | 0.896 | 0.0 |
| Only HE | 0.830 ± 0.014 | 0.801 ± 0.017 | 0.896 | 2.5 |
| Firdaus BC-FL-HE | 0.833 ± 0.026 | 0.801 ± 0.033 | 0.892 | 2.2 |
| Hybrid (Lite) | 0.833 ± 0.026 | 0.801 ± 0.033 | 0.892 | 6.3 |
| Only FL | 0.837 ± 0.018 | 0.808 ± 0.023 | 0.896 | 0.1 |

**Paired t-test, Hybrid (Lite) vs Firdaus BC-FL-HE:** t = 0.000,
p = 1.000, mean_diff = 0.0000, n = 5 — *not significant (identical
per-seed outputs)*. On clean data the Hybrid gates reject nothing
(rej_norm = rej_acc = rej_rep = 0) and the DP noise (σ = 0.001) is
negligible, so Hybrid (Lite) reproduces Firdaus bit-for-bit on every
seed. The all-zero paired differences mean the t-statistic is formally
0/0; we report it as a non-significant zero difference rather than NaN.
The defenses do not hurt accuracy on clean data.

**Plot:** `multiseed_cleveland.png`.

**One-line reading:** all variants overlap within one std. Hybrid
matches Firdaus exactly. **Adding our 3 new gates costs no accuracy.**

## Section E3 — DLG (Deep Leakage from Gradients) attack

[section_E3_dlg/](section_E3_dlg/) — quantitative gradient-inversion
attack across DP-noise σ.

| Framework | DP σ | cos similarity (rec, true) | feature MSE | label recovery |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Plaintext FL (no defense) | 0.0 | +0.387 | 0.848 | 70% |
| Firdaus (HE only — insider sees decrypt) | 0.0 | +0.387 | 0.848 | 70% |
| Hybrid (DP σ=0.001, current default) | 0.001 | +0.387 | 0.848 | 70% |
| Hybrid (DP σ=0.01) | 0.01 | +0.387 | 0.853 | 70% |
| Hybrid (DP σ=0.1) | 0.1 | +0.375 | 0.974 | 70% |
| **Hybrid (DP σ=0.5)** | 0.5 | **+0.090** | **2.957** | 50% |
| **Hybrid (DP σ=1.0)** | 1.0 | **−0.036** | **5.626** | **40%** |
| Hybrid (DP σ=5.0) | 5.0 | −0.022 | 28.260 | 50% |

**Plot:** `dlg_curve.png`.

**One-line reading:** at our default σ = 0.001 DP is **inadequate**
against DLG; reconstruction breaks at **σ ≥ 0.5** (cos drops 4×, MSE
3.5×). Concrete production recommendation, with measured cost.

## Section E4 — Multi-attacker collusion (UCI Cleveland, single seed)

[section_E4_collusion/](section_E4_collusion/) — three frameworks
under three adversarial fractions.

| Attacker fraction | Only FL | Firdaus | **Hybrid** | Δ (Hybrid − Firdaus) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1/4 (25%) | 0.65 | 0.80 | **0.85** | **+5pp** |
| **2/4 (50%)** | 0.52 | 0.83 | **0.90** | **+7pp** ⭐ |
| 3/4 (75%) | 0.60 | 0.80 | 0.80 | tied (formally hopeless) |

Notes for 2/4 case: Hybrid catches 58/60 attacker submissions
(rej_norm=6, rej_acc=1, rej_rep=51); Firdaus only rejects 13.

**Plot:** `collusion.png`.

**One-line reading:** **the strongest empirical win.** At 50% colluding
attackers, Hybrid beats Firdaus by 7pp and Only-FL by 38pp. The 75%
case is formally hopeless (no aggregation rule can recover from
majority-Byzantine).

## Three numbers to remember

1. **+7pp** — accuracy gap of Hybrid over Firdaus at 50% colluding
   attackers (Section E4). Strongest empirical win.
2. **f = 1** — Byzantine fault tolerance proven via PBFT (Section D).
   Neither review paper has this.
3. **σ ≥ 0.5** — DP-noise threshold required to break gradient-
   inversion (Section E3). Concrete production recommendation.

These three numbers, plus the architectural integration of Stages 1-5
that no prior paper achieves, constitute the empirically defensible
noble contribution of this thesis.
